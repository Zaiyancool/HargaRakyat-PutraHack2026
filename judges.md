### ❓ FAQs

> *Got questions? Find quick answers to the most common queries about team formation, rules, tools, and event logistics.*

> 

- **Who can join?**

    

    The event is open to all students currently enrolled in Malaysian Institutions of Higher Learning (IPT), including both public and private universities. Participants must be at least 18 years old by the year 2026. Individuals in privileged roles, such as organizers, judges, mentors, and partner representatives, are not eligible to participate.

    

- **Can I join without a team?**

    

    Yes, absolutely! You can participate as a solo hacker. The rules state that teams can consist of 1 to 4 members. If you participate alone, you will simply register as a "team of one" and act as your own Team Leader. Please note that the organisers will not randomly match solo participants into groups, so you are good to hack on your own!

    

- **What should I bring?**

    

    For the Grand Finale, bring your laptop, chargers, student ID, and any presentation materials you need. Please note that bringing any items that could be considered dangerous or harmful is strictly forbidden.

    

- **Can I sleep at the venue?**

    

    The physical Grand Finale on April 11th is a daytime event running from 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM, so overnight sleeping at the venue is not applicable.

    

- **What happens if I miss the deadline?**

    

    Late or incomplete submissions will not be considered. The organizers are not responsible for submission failures caused by internet issues, platform downtime, or device malfunctions on your end. We strongly advise you to submit early!

    

- **Where do I submit my submissions?**

    

    Submissions must be made online through the designated submission platform, which will be announced via the official Discord channel. If you advance to the Grand Finale, refined materials must be submitted through the specific Grand Finale Rounds Submission Form.

    

- **Can we use AI tools?**

    

    Participants are encouraged to use AI-powered coding assistants and research tools. However, to ensure fair evaluation, all teams must use the Official Tooling Suite approved by PutraHack 2026. 

    

    Specialized “end-to-end” generation platforms — other than our official partner, **Lovable** — are restricted to ensure all projects are evaluated on a comparable technical baseline. You must also disclose all AI tools used during development by clearly stating the tool's name and its purpose in your Pitch Deck.

    

    Participants **must** use **Lovable** in at least one main submission, either for prototyping or deployment of their web app. Simply mentioning Lovable in pitch decks or videos is **not sufficient.**

    Examples of approved tools include ChatGPT/OpenAI API, OpenClaw, GitHub Copilot, Claude Code, Gemini, Perplexity, and Cursor/Antigravity.

    

    **Please note that PutraHack may update the list if needed. Always follow announcements in official Discord channels for the latest guidance.*
